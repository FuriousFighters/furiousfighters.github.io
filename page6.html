Furious Fighters Blog

Written by: Jason Raquel and Reynald Tenorio

There are hundreds of martial arts styles in the world, but this website focuses only on two famous styles from the Orient. Furious Fighters aims to differentiate the two popular martial arts, namely Karate and Taekwondo. This will contain information about them, from their basic techniques to their professional aspects. This blog will surely be of use to martial arts practitioners and can serve as a starting point to those who are interested. It can also serve as a center of discussion as long as it is related to martial arts and teaching new and better techniques, master practitioners can share their thoughts and knowledge to this site to widen and improve this online community. We will also answer questions to the best of our abilities as one of the developers is actually a practitioner of karate.
This blog will answer all curiosity about martial arts,
Martial arts are not used for violence but acts as a self-defense and requires discipline. A practitioner should learn how to control themselves and never use this knowledge as a way to hurt or bully people.  It takes time and determination to master a technique, it cannot happen instantly. Although this is the truth, all people can learn martial arts but those who have passion and are serious on training will really succeed. Even masters do not stop training to improve more or maintain their mastery of a certain style. There is no doubt that training is hard but as you progress, you will realize that it gets easier in the end if you keep on doing it just like exercise. 
There are many kinds of reasons to learn martial arts. Some do it for self-defense, some came to discipline themselves and some went to improve their physical traits (reaction time, speed, strength, power, stamina, resistance). This is nothing like team sports that needs to rely on each other to succeed. In learning martial arts, you should not depend on another to improve your skill, although it is good to have your co-practitioners at your side to guide you, in the end it will mainly depend on your own skill and determination. Learning martial arts prepares you during any fight situations and gives you natural instincts that automatically makes you handle hard situations more easily. There are many practitioners, millions even, of martial arts but do not waver, as they do not have the exact same traits as you. Martial arts is not only about  strength or power, what is the use of brute strength if it does not land on the opponent? You should learn to balance your physical skills to avoid being countered. People think that learning martial arts is easy, but it is not so. Its goal is to know the true meaning of martial arts. Until now, there are people, especially the practitioners themselves, who says that a style is better than others, because it is the style they are practicing. The famous practitioner of martial arts in 1970's, Bruce Lee said that he is not proving that his styles are not superior to others, and no matter what kind of style you practiced it does not matter that much. There is no superior martial arts technique, it depends on how you use it.
